About Provo, Utah 2

Provo, Utah is named after one of the earliest known french trappers to have trapped the area, Etienne Provost. Pioneers from the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints were the first non-Native Americans to settle the area. When a railroad connected Provo to Salt Lake City in 1873, Provo was able to become an industry and commerce center.

Provo, Utah is home to Brigham Young University (BYU). BYU often times referred to as BYU Provo (to prevent confusion with BYU-Idaho or BYU-Hawaii) is a private university run by the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints.
